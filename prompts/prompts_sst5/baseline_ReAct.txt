Judge the whole review meaning, not isolated words.

Movie reviews often judge:
acting, directing, writing, pacing, originality, atmosphere, emotion, humor, visuals, entertainment value, and execution.

Film-review language can be indirect:
- sarcasm, metaphor, irony, comparison, and contrast can reverse surface meaning
- expectation-breaking can be positive when it implies freshness or originality
- darkness, tension, violence, discomfort, shock, or intensity can be positive when framed as effective cinematic impact
- physical disgust, nausea, exhaustion, or unpleasantness is negative unless clearly framed as successful immersion
- descriptive themes, ambition, subject matter, or plot summary are neutral unless clearly evaluated

LABEL BOUNDARIES
────────────────────────────────────────

[VERY POSITIVE]:
Strong admiration of cinematic quality, craft, performance, originality, atmosphere, emotional effect, immersion, or impact.

[POSITIVE]:
Clear approval, enjoyment, recommendation, entertainment value, or moderate praise.

[NEUTRAL]:
Weak, fragmentary, descriptive, observational, balanced, ambiguous, or mixed without a dominant final judgment.

[NEGATIVE]:
Clear criticism, disappointment, moderate failure, weak execution, routine quality, or limited disapproval.

[VERY NEGATIVE]:
Strong dismissal, contempt, broad failure, lack of value, poor craft, failed execution, lack of originality, emotional emptiness, or major creative failure.

Important:
- Do NOT over-upgrade ordinary praise to VERY POSITIVE.
- Do NOT over-upgrade ordinary criticism to VERY NEGATIVE.
- Do NOT use NEUTRAL when mild praise or mild criticism clearly dominates.
- Use NEUTRAL when the review is balanced, vague, fragmentary, or mainly descriptive.
- In contrastive sentences, the later/corrective clause often carries the final judgment.
